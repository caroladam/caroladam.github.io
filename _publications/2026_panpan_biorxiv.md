excerpt: 'We sequence and assemble 58 haplotypes from four distinct Pan clades to high contiguity, including eight near-T2T genomes. We find that structural variants (SVs) are 170- to 260-fold more likely than single nucleotide variants (SNVs) to exhibit high-impact effects across species, and that human disease-associated tandem repeats (TRs) are expanded and highly diverse in humans, sensitizing our species to TR-expansion disorders.'
